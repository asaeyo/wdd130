History

Whitewater rafting has its roots in the mid-19th century, when rubber rafts were first employed by military expeditions to explore uncharted rivers. It wasn't until the 1970s, however, that the activity gained popularity as a recreational sport. Since then, whitewater rafting has significantly evolved, thanks to advancement in safety measures and equipments- making the thrill of the rapids accessible to a much wider audience.

The term"whitewater" refers to the frothy, fast-moving water created as a river flows over rocks, sudden drops and other natural obstacles. Rapids are classified by difficulty on a scale from Class I (easy and gentle) to Class V (extremely challenging and technical), offering options for every skill level- from beginners to seasoned adventurers. At white water rafting, expert guidance is provided from experienced instructors. What began as a modest means of exploration has since grown into a globally popular activity, attracting adventure enthusiasts from all corners of the world.
